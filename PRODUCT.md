# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Carter and a small, trusted circle of family and friends — self-directed retail investors who manage their own money. They hold across several real accounts (Fidelity brokerage, Roth IRA, HYSA, checking) and think in positions, sectors, and macro context, not just balances.

Their context when using Fintrack: a quick daily read on where the portfolio stands and what's moving, plus occasional deeper sessions — reviewing holdings, scanning market movers and the forward event calendar, checking options/futures, reading position-relevant news, and testing ideas in a paper account before committing real capital. They are comfortable with financial density; they are not day-trading professionals and don't want a terminal that fights them.

## Product Purpose

Fintrack consolidates multiple brokerage and bank accounts into one calm, precise dashboard: live prices, holdings and allocation, a market summary (movers, earnings), a forward-looking macro + holdings calendar, options and futures views, position news, and a paper-trading sandbox.

It exists because the alternatives don't fit: pro terminals are overwhelming and expensive, consumer apps gamify and oversimplify, and a spreadsheet can't show live market context. Success is the user opening Fintrack first thing, getting an honest at-a-glance read in seconds, and trusting every number on the screen.

## Brand Personality

Precise, dark, restrained — a precision instrument, not a toy. "James Bond" composure: quiet confidence, exacting detail, nothing flashy. Amber-on-near-black, semantic color used sparingly. The voice is plain and exact: it states what a number is and what changed, never hypes. Calm under volatility — the interface should feel steady even when the market isn't.

## Anti-references

- **Robinhood gamification** — no confetti, streaks, dopamine bait, or trading-as-a-game patterns. Investing is treated seriously.
- **Generic bank-template dashboards** — not sterile, corporate, or stock-photo bland. This has a point of view.
- **Mint-style pastels** — no soft consumer-budgeting palette. Color is semantic (gain / loss / brand), not decorative cheer.

(Dense, pro-grade information design is welcome — the goal is precision without the clutter, not dumbing down.)

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over noise.** Every figure on screen earns its place. Surface the read first; let depth live one click away in the tabs. Density is fine when it's all signal.
2. **Calm confidence.** The interface reassures rather than agitates — no urgency theater, no blinking, no manufactured FOMO. Steadiness is the feature.
3. **Honest data.** Show real precision only when it's real. Mock/placeholder data is clearly provisional; nothing fakes accuracy it doesn't have. Trust is the whole product.
4. **Restraint as luxury.** Color, motion, and ornament are rationed. Amber is the brand, emerald/ruby are gain/loss, everything else is near-black and ink. What's left out is the craft.
5. **One glance, then depth.** The dashboard answers "where do I stand?" instantly; each tab rewards a deeper look without making the glance harder.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 against its surface (already verified in the OKLCH token set). Gain/loss and other states are never conveyed by color alone — sign, value, and label carry the meaning too, for color-blind users. Full keyboard navigation and visible focus on all interactive controls. Every animation honors `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade or instant fallback. Dark-only by design; contrast is tuned for that single, intended environment.
